I earned a PhD in the history of science in 2013, then took the alt-academic
route: from digital humanities projects into instructional technology, and
from there into systems administration for a university education-services
organization.

My historical research centers on eighteenth-century chemistry — Joseph
Black's lectures and correspondence, the chemical communities of the Scottish
Enlightenment, and, more recently, how the era's science was entangled with
slavery and empire.
